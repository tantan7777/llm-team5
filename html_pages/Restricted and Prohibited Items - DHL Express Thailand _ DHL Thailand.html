Prohibited items for international shipping with DHL Express

January 22, 2025 5 Mins Read
Share on
Share

Have you ever wondered what can and cannot be shipped internationally? Shipping internationally requires a thorough understanding of what is permitted and what is not. Navigating the complexities of international shipping is essential to ensure smooth and timely delivery, avoid unexpected delays, and maintain compliance with global regulations. This guide offers a comprehensive overview of prohibited and restricted items when shipping with DHL Express in Thailand, helping you to navigate the process with confidence and ease. 

What are prohibited items to ship internationally?

Prohibited items are strictly forbidden from shipping under any circumstances. These items pose significant risks and are not allowed, regardless of the destination or shipping method. Examples of DHL Express’ prohibited items include:

  • Bullion: Gold, silver, and platinum bullion are considered valuable commodities and are prohibited items to ship internationally unless properly declared and documented for customs purposes.
  • Currency: Shipping large amounts of currency generally requires explicit authorization due to potential financial and security risks.
  • Animals: Most live animals are prohibited from shipping internationally, with very few exceptions for scientific or regulated purposes, ensuring ethical and legal transportation.
  • Human remains: Shipping human remains necessitates special permits, strict compliance with handling procedures, and sensitive handling throughout the shipping process. Non-compliance may lead to legal actions.
  • Counterfeit goods: These are illegal and are strictly prohibited, leading to severe penalties if shipped.
  • Drugs: Illegal drugs and controlled substances are strictly prohibited from shipping under any circumstances due to the serious legal and safety implications.
  • Hazardous materials: Explosives, flammable liquids, toxic substances, and other hazardous materials require specialized handling and may require permits or licenses.

Shipping dangerous goods internationally from or to Thailand falls under strict scrutiny. Failure to adhere to these rules can result in shipment rejections, penalties, or legal actions.

What are restricted items? 

Restricted items are those that may require special approval or additional documentation for shipping. These items can still be shipped, but only under specific conditions such as providing proper certifications, adhering to safety standards, or ensuring that proper handling and packaging are in place to minimize risks.

Examples of restricted items include:

  • Antiques: These often require permits and documentation to preserve their historical or cultural value and to comply with regulations that safeguard their integrity.
  • Dry ice: A hazardous material that demands precise packaging and handling to avoid accidents or damage to surrounding goods.
  • Alcoholic beverages: Depending on the destination country, these may require licenses, specific labeling, and adherence to customs regulations.
  • Perfumes: As flammable liquids, perfumes require specialized handling and packaging to meet stringent safety requirements.

To ship restricted items with DHL Express, customers must provide the necessary documentation, undergo thorough inspections, and ensure that proper packaging is used to meet international shipping standards. For those looking to ship items like lithium batteries or other dangerous goods internationally with DHL Express, understanding and adhering to these requirements is crucial for safe and compliant delivery.

How does DHL identify prohibited or restricted Items?

DHL Express has a stringent process in place to identify prohibited and restricted items when shipping internationally from and to Thailand. Various circumstances may trigger inspections to ensure compliance with shipping regulations:

  • Odor or signs of leakage: If a package emits a suspicious odor or shows signs of leakage, DHL Express may conduct an inspection to determine if it contains prohibited or restricted goods. This is crucial to avoid potential damage to other shipments and maintain safety standards.
  • Inappropriate packaging: Packages that are improperly packaged, damaged, or tampered with may be subject to inspection to ensure that they meet international shipping standards. Proper packaging of goods for shipping is essential to minimize the risk of mishandling or contamination.
  • Suspicious package shape: Unusual shapes or sizes of packages may raise concerns and prompt an inspection. This ensures that the contents align with DHL’s guidelines for safe and secure transportation.

Consequences of shipping prohibited or restricted items

Shipping prohibited or restricted items can lead to severe consequences, including:

  • No insurance or liability coverage: DHL Express may refuse to provide insurance or liability coverage for shipments that contain prohibited or restricted items. This means any loss or damage will not be compensated.
  • Refusal of package pickup: If a package is found to contain prohibited or restricted items, DHL may refuse to pick it up altogether.
  • Destruction or return of goods: In some cases, DHL may opt to destroy or return prohibited or restricted items at the sender’s expense.
  • Potential penalties and legal action: Shipping illegal or hazardous items can result in significant penalties, fines, and even legal consequences, including criminal charges in extreme cases.

These measures ensure that DHL Express maintains a safe and compliant shipping environment for all parties involved.

How to avoid transporting restricted or prohibited Items

To ensure a smooth and compliant shipping experience, customers should follow these steps:

  • Check destination country regulations: Each country has its own specific rules regarding what can and cannot be shipped. Customers should thoroughly review these customs regulations for permissible and restricted items in Thailand to avoid any issues when shipping internationally.
  • Partner with shipping experts: DHL Express offers a team of experienced shipping experts who can provide guidance on shipping restricted or prohibited items. They can help customers navigate complex regulations and ensure compliance with international shipping standards.
  • Utilize DHL Express' online resources: Customers can access DHL’s online resources, including shipping guides. For instance, international guides like shipping from Thailand to the USA offer valuable insights. Additionally, tools for estimating shipping rates and delivery times help customers plan more effectively.

Other key considerations for shipping with DHL Express include: 

  1. Packaging and Labeling: Proper packaging plays a significant role in ensuring the safety of goods. Poorly packaged items may be subjected to additional scrutiny, leading to potential rejection or damage during transit.
  2. Customs Documentation: Accurate and complete customs documentation is essential for a smooth shipping experience. This includes providing detailed descriptions of the contents, proper valuation, and adhering to documentation requirements set by the destination country.
  3. Shipping Dangerous Goods: For hazardous materials, DHL Express provides specialized services to ensure safe and compliant transport. 

By adhering to these practices and utilizing DHL Express’s resources, businesses and individuals can achieve safe, efficient, and hassle-free international shipping.
